History of Cats

Touch points

  • Cats have been companions to humans for thousands of years. The first evidence of domesticated cats dates back around 9,000 years in the Near East, where wildcats were attracted to human settlements for the plentiful food. Ancient Egyptians famously revered cats, considering them sacred and protecting them in law. They were symbols of grace, fertility, and protection, and many households kept cats to control pests.
  • Cats spread through Europe and Asia over centuries, often traveling on ships to control rodents. In the Middle Ages, their reputation suffered due to superstition, but they remained beloved companions. Today, cats are one of the world’s most popular pets, celebrated for their independence, playful personalities, and unique charm.

Early Domestication

Cats were first domesticated around 7500–7000 BCE in the Fertile Crescent (modern-day Middle East). Humans began to settle and farm, and wildcats were drawn to stored grain, which attracted mice. Cats in Ancient Egypt

Ancient Egyptians elevated cats to a sacred status. They were associated with goddesses like Bastet, the goddess of home, fertility, and protection. Cats were often depicted in art and even mummified alongside their owners. Harming a cat was considered a serious crime, and their presence in homes was both practical and spiritual.

Cats Spread Around the World

Cats traveled with traders, merchants, and sailors. By 500 CE, they were common in Europe and Asia. They were especially valued on ships for controlling rats and mice. During this time, cats began to develop the variety of breeds and physical traits we recognize today.

About Us
